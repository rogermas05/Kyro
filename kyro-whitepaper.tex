\begin{abstract}
\noindent
Kyro is an on-chain trade finance protocol that converts real-world invoices into yield-bearing
instruments on ADI Chain. SMEs tokenize invoices to receive instant stablecoin liquidity;
institutional investors deposit into an ERC-4626 vault and earn real yield as invoices settle.
The protocol enforces KYC at the token-transfer level via an on-chain identity registry,
structures each invoice into senior (80\%) and junior (20\%) tranches, and includes a
deployed ERC-4337 account abstraction layer designed to remove wallet and gas complexity
for non-crypto-native users. This document describes
the architecture deployed on the ADI testnet.
\end{abstract}

\section{Motivation and Design}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Global trade finance has a \$2+ trillion annual funding gap. SMEs holding valid invoices against
creditworthy buyers cannot access liquidity without slow, expensive factoring intermediaries.
Simultaneously, institutional investors seeking yield on stablecoin holdings lack transparent,
on-chain instruments with well-defined risk profiles.

Kyro solves both problems: SMEs tokenize invoices and receive DDSC (UAE Dirham stablecoin)
within a single block; investors earn real yield as those invoices are repaid. Three principles
govern the design: \textbf{compliance-first} (KYC enforced at the EVM level, not the application
layer); \textbf{standard interfaces} (ERC-20, ERC-721, ERC-4626, ERC-4337 throughout); and
\textbf{simplicity for end users} (ERC-4337 account abstraction is built to remove
wallet and gas friction, with paymaster integration planned for production).

\section{System Architecture}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Kyro is organized as four tightly integrated layers plus a merchant payment module.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering\small
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{lX}
\toprule
\textbf{Layer} & \textbf{Contracts} \\
\midrule
1 — Identity \& Compliance  & \texttt{IdentityRegistry}, \texttt{ClaimTopicsRegistry}, \texttt{TrustedIssuersRegistry} \\
2 — Asset Orchestration     & \texttt{InvoiceOrchestrator}, \texttt{InvoiceToken} (ERC-721), \texttt{SeniorToken}, \texttt{JuniorToken} (ERC-20), \texttt{InvoiceZKVerifier} \\
3 — Liquidity \& Yield      & \texttt{TradeFinanceVault} (ERC-4626), \texttt{MockDDSC} stablecoin \\
4 — Account Abstraction     & \texttt{SimpleSmartAccount}, \texttt{SimpleSmartAccountFactory}, \texttt{SignaturePaymaster}, \texttt{MinimalEntryPoint} (ERC-4337 v0.7) \\
Merchant Module             & \texttt{ADIPayRouter}, \texttt{PriceOracle}, \texttt{MockSwapRouter} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

\vspace{-2pt}
\noindent\textbf{Key parameters:} senior tranche = 80\% face value (S-DEBT tokens); junior
tranche = 20\% (J-DEBT tokens); senior yield target = 6\% annualized; stablecoin = DDSC
(1 DDSC $\approx$ 1 AED); network = ADI testnet, Chain ID 99999.

\subsection{Layer 1 — Identity \& Compliance}

\texttt{IdentityRegistry} is the source of truth for participant eligibility. Every address
maps to a record of \{\texttt{registered}, \texttt{kycApproved}, \texttt{country}\}. The view
function \texttt{isVerified(address)} returns \texttt{true} only when both flags are set.

Compliance is enforced by overriding the \texttt{\_update()} hook in every transfer-restricted
token. Any transfer to an unverified recipient reverts unconditionally at the EVM level — no
application layer can bypass it. Minting (from zero) and burning (to zero) are unrestricted.

Two roles govern the registry: \texttt{ADMIN\_ROLE} (configures the registry) and
\texttt{COMPLIANCE\_AGENT\_ROLE} (registers addresses and manages KYC status). On testnet,
the deployer holds both roles and auto-approves wallets that interact with the protocol.

\subsection{Layer 2 — Asset Orchestration}

\texttt{InvoiceOrchestrator} is the single entry point for all invoice operations and holds
MINTER roles over all three token contracts. When an SME submits an invoice, the orchestrator:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em, topsep=2pt, itemsep=1pt]
  \item Verifies the SME is KYC-approved via \texttt{IdentityRegistry.isVerified()}.
  \item Validates the attestation signature via \texttt{InvoiceZKVerifier} (ECDSA oracle check).
  \item Mints an \texttt{InvoiceToken} NFT to itself (escrow — never transferred to users).
  \item Mints $0.80 \times \texttt{faceValue}$ S-DEBT to itself for vault purchase.
  \item Mints $0.20 \times \texttt{faceValue}$ J-DEBT directly to the SME.
  \item Records the invoice in state as \texttt{PENDING}.
\end{enumerate}

All six operations are atomic. Invoice state transitions: \textbf{PENDING} $\to$
\textbf{ACTIVE} (on vault purchase) $\to$ \textbf{SETTLED} or \textbf{DEFAULTED}.

Each invoice NFT stores: face value, due date, document hash (keccak256 of the off-chain
document), counterparty address, and SME address. Only the hash is on-chain; the actual
invoice document remains off-chain, satisfying commercial confidentiality requirements.
Replay attacks are prevented by rejecting duplicate invoice IDs.

\textbf{Settlement:} when the counterparty repays, a settlement oracle calls
\texttt{settleInvoice()}. DDSC (principal + accrued interest) is forwarded to the vault,
all tokens are burned, and the invoice transitions to SETTLED.

\textbf{Default:} if the due date passes without repayment, the oracle calls
\texttt{defaultInvoice()}. J-DEBT is burned entirely (first-loss wipeout for the SME),
any recovered DDSC goes to the vault up to the senior face value, and the invoice
transitions to DEFAULTED. The 80/20 structure protects the vault from losses unless
recovery is below 80\% of face value.

\subsection{Layer 3 — Liquidity \& Yield Vault}

\texttt{TradeFinanceVault} is a standard ERC-4626 tokenized vault over DDSC. The two
core quantities are:
\[
  \texttt{totalAssets()} = \text{DDSC cash} + \text{S-DEBT held (at par)}
  \qquad
  \texttt{sharePrice} = \frac{\texttt{totalAssets()}}{\texttt{totalSupply()}}
\]

When the vault purchases a senior tranche, DDSC decreases and S-DEBT increases by the same
amount — \texttt{totalAssets()} is unchanged, so the share price does not move. The share
price rises \emph{only} when interest arrives at settlement. Yield accrual is automatic and
passive; investors do not need to claim rewards.

Vault shares are KYC-gated via the same \texttt{\_update()} hook pattern. Only verified
investors may receive or transfer shares. DDSC itself is unrestricted.

\subsection{Layer 4 — Account Abstraction}

The ERC-4337 contract suite is fully deployed: \texttt{SimpleSmartAccount} (deterministic
CREATE2 wallet, ECDSA-validated), \texttt{SimpleSmartAccountFactory} (computes wallet
addresses before deployment so compliance agents can KYC-register an address ahead of
its first use), \texttt{SignaturePaymaster} (sponsors gas on behalf of approved accounts),
and \texttt{MinimalEntryPoint} (coordinates UserOp validation and execution).

On the current testnet deployment, the frontend uses standard MetaMask EOA wallets rather
than smart accounts — users sign transactions directly and pay gas in ADI. The account
abstraction layer is architectural infrastructure ready for integration: connecting the
paymaster would allow SMEs to interact without holding native ADI tokens, and batched
UserOps would make multi-step flows (e.g., approve + mintInvoice) atomic.

\subsection{Merchant Module — KyroPay}

\texttt{ADIPayRouter} provides fiat-denominated crypto checkout. A merchant specifies a price
in AED; the \texttt{PriceOracle} converts it to token units; the \texttt{MockSwapRouter}
executes any required token-to-token swap; the merchant receives their preferred token.
A QR code workflow encodes the full checkout configuration for mobile wallets.

\section{End-to-End Flow}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=2em, topsep=2pt, itemsep=2pt, label=\textbf{\arabic*.}]
  \item \textbf{KYC.} Compliance agent registers the SME and investor wallets in
    \texttt{IdentityRegistry}.
  \item \textbf{Invoice Minting.} SME submits an invoice form. The frontend calls the
    attestation API (oracle signs the invoice digest), then the SME's MetaMask wallet
    signs and sends a \texttt{mintInvoice()} transaction (gas paid in ADI). The SME
    receives J-DEBT (20\% of face value). Invoice is PENDING.
  \item \textbf{Investor Deposit.} Institution calls \texttt{vault.deposit(DDSC, amount)}.
    Vault mints shares at the current share price.
  \item \textbf{Tranche Purchase.} Vault operator calls \texttt{purchaseSeniorTranche()}.
    Vault sends DDSC to the SME and receives S-DEBT. Invoice transitions to ACTIVE.
    \texttt{totalAssets()} unchanged; share price unchanged.
  \item \textbf{Settlement.} Counterparty repays. Oracle calls \texttt{settleInvoice()}.
    Vault receives principal + interest. \texttt{totalAssets()} rises; share price rises.
  \item \textbf{Redemption.} Investor calls \texttt{vault.redeem()}. Receives DDSC at the
    new (higher) share price — the difference is their yield.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Frontend and API}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

The frontend is a Next.js 14 application using Viem for on-chain interactions. Three portals:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em, topsep=2pt, itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textbf{SME portal} (\texttt{/sme}): invoice submission form with PDF/image auto-parsing
    (Claude vision API), attestation flow, and a live invoice dashboard showing all invoices by
    state with fund, settle, and default actions.
  \item \textbf{Investor portal} (\texttt{/investor}): vault performance panel (total assets,
    share price, deployed vs. idle DDSC), deposit and redeem cards, and an invoice management
    panel for funding and settling open invoices.
  \item \textbf{Auditor portal} (\texttt{/auditor}): read-only event log querying
    \texttt{InvoiceMinted}, \texttt{InvoiceSettled}, and \texttt{InvoiceDefaulted} events
    from the last 10,000 blocks. No wallet required.
\end{itemize}

\noindent Backend API routes (Next.js server functions) handle oracle attestation, KYC
registration, tranche funding, settlement, default, and a testnet faucet. All privileged
operations use a deployer key stored in environment variables.

\section{Security and Access Control}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

All privileged operations are protected by OpenZeppelin \texttt{AccessControl}.
Key roles: \texttt{COMPLIANCE\_AGENT\_ROLE} (KYC management), \texttt{MINTER\_ROLE}
(token mint/burn, held only by the orchestrator), \texttt{VAULT\_ROLE} (tranche purchase),
\texttt{SETTLEMENT\_ROLE} (settle/default), and \texttt{OPERATOR\_ROLE} (paymaster sponsorship).
On testnet, the deployer holds all roles.

The orchestrator follows checks-effects-interactions: all state updates occur before
external calls, preventing re-entrancy. OpenZeppelin \texttt{ReentrancyGuard} is applied
to vault deposit and redeem paths as defense-in-depth. Invoice uniqueness is enforced by
a mapping that reverts on duplicate IDs, making ZK proof replay impossible.

\subsection*{Testnet Simplifications}

This deployment is intentionally simplified for demonstration. In production, the following
components would be upgraded:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=2em, topsep=2pt, itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textbf{KYC}: currently auto-approved; production requires document verification and
    AML screening.
  \item \textbf{Oracle attestation}: currently signs any well-formed request; production would
    verify invoices against trade databases and buyer credit scores, and use a decentralized
    signing committee or formally verified ZK proving system.
  \item \textbf{Settlement}: currently triggered manually via API; production would use
    time-locked automatic settlement with on-chain oracle confirmation.
  \item \textbf{Price oracle}: currently uses fixed rates; production would integrate
    Chainlink aggregators with staleness checks.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Kyro demonstrates that institutional-grade trade finance can be implemented entirely on-chain
without sacrificing compliance, privacy, or user experience. The four-layer architecture —
identity, asset orchestration, yield vault, and account abstraction — is modular by design:
each layer can be independently upgraded, audited, and composed with other DeFi protocols.

The protocol achieves compliance without centralization (KYC at the token level),
privacy without opacity (only document hashes on-chain), yield without complexity
(automatic ERC-4626 share price appreciation), and a deployed ERC-4337 layer ready
to abstract wallet and gas complexity for non-crypto-native users.

The testnet deployment is a functional end-to-end proof of concept. The architecture
is designed to accommodate production-grade oracle integrations, multi-jurisdiction
compliance regimes, and per-invoice parameterized tranche splits as the protocol
matures toward mainnet.
